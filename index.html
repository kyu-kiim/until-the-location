Chapter 01 — Departure

The coordinates were wrong from the beginning. She had known this even before the map loaded, before the blinking cursor settled over a place that no longer existed on any official record. Distance was not the problem. The problem was the address itself — a number assigned to an empty lot, a ghost of a building.

Chapter 02 — The Road

Asphalt gives way to gravel, then to dirt, then to nothing at all. The route the algorithm suggested had not accounted for the season. It had not accounted for anything, really — only the straight-line logic of point A to point B, indifferent to weather and time and the condition of the ground.

Chapter 03 — Signal

One bar. Then none. The screen showed a map frozen in the last moment of connection — a blue dot hovering over unmarked terrain. She kept moving anyway, because the direction was still clear even if the path was not. That is perhaps all navigation ever is.

Chapter 04 — Landmark

There was a structure — not quite a building, not quite a ruin. It appeared where the road bent sharply east. The satellite imagery had shown nothing here. That meant either the imagery was old, or the structure had been built in secret, or both of these things at once.

Chapter 05 — Distance

Twelve kilometers, the device said. Then eleven. The number changed, but the landscape did not. She began to suspect the measurement was tracking something other than her movement through space. Perhaps time. Perhaps the distance between the self that began this journey and whatever self was arriving.

Chapter 06 — Night

Darkness arrived before she expected it. The display turned blue, then gray. The battery icon appeared in the corner like a small warning flag. She turned the brightness down to extend whatever was left. Navigation by diminishing light, which is to say: most navigation.

Chapter 07 — Arrival

You have arrived at your destination. The voice said it twice, as if uncertain. She looked at what was in front of her. It was not what she had been looking for. It was something else — which is the usual outcome. The destination names a direction more than a place.

Chapter 08 — After

She stood for a long time without moving. The device timed out and the screen went dark. In the silence the distance she had crossed became real to her — not as kilometers but as duration, as effort, as the particular quality of attention required to keep moving when the path is unclear.
