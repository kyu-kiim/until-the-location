Until The Location

Where do you stand now? Can your coordinates express you? Or is it simply a False abstraction? Your coordinates are the Consequences Produced by a much more complex path than you think. That doesn’t mean where you actually stand.

Simply put, the coordinates presented by GPS appear to be the simplest way to describe the current location. However, the coordinates are the result of omitting the actual sense of place, the reason for movement, the time of stay, the surroundings, and the path to reach it. Location is not immediately given by a single number but is determined temporarily through signal reception and loss, reference point setting, error correction, and data conversion.

How many processes do you have to go through before you become a position? How many errors and obstructions, attempts to correct them, location is like a countless repetitive battle rather than an immediate determination. You participate in the battle while being dataized and pressed flat. You are marked on the map with a single dot, with your senses excluded.

Natural Disturbance

Twelve kilometers, the device said. Then eleven. The number changed, but the landscape did not. She began to suspect the measurement was tracking something other than her movement through space. Perhaps time. Perhaps the distance between the self that began this journey and whatever self was arriving.

Artificial Disturbance

Darkness arrived before she expected it. The display turned blue, then gray. The battery icon appeared in the corner like a small warning flag. She turned the brightness down to extend whatever was left. Navigation by diminishing light, which is to say: most navigation.

You have arrived at your destination. The voice said it twice, as if uncertain. She looked at what was in front of her. It was not what she had been looking for. It was something else — which is the usual outcome. The destination names a direction more than a place.

Index

She stood for a long time without moving. The device timed out and the screen went dark. In the silence the distance she had crossed became real to her — not as kilometers but as duration, as effort, as the particular quality of attention required to keep moving when the path is unclear.
